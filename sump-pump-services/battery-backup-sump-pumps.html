Frequently Asked Questions

A battery backup sump pump is an auxiliary pump system that operates using battery power, providing emergency water removal from a sump pit when the primary electric sump pump fails, typically due to power outages or mechanical issues.

The battery for a backup sump pump typically lasts between 5 to 12 hours of continuous use, depending on the capacity and efficiency of the battery. Regular checks and maintenance can ensure optimal performance.

Yes, many homeowners with basic plumbing and electrical skills can install a battery backup sump pump themselves by following the manufacturer's instructions. However, professional installation is recommended to ensure proper setup and function.

Regular maintenance includes checking the battery's charge, cleaning the pump and pit, testing the pump periodically, and replacing the battery every 2-5 years depending on usage and battery type.

The size of a battery backup sump pump depends on the sump pit size, the water volume you expect to move, and the height the water needs to be lifted. It's best to consult the manufacturer's guidelines or a professional to determine the appropriate pump size for your needs.
