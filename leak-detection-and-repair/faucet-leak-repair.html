Frequently Asked Questions

Faucet leaks are usually caused by worn-out washers or O-rings, corroded valve seats, or loose parts. Mineral deposits can also build up and cause leaks.

To fix a leaking faucet, turn off the water supply, disassemble the faucet, inspect components for damage, replace worn parts like washers or seals, and reassemble the faucet. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions or consult a professional if unsure.

Basic tools needed for faucet leak repair include a screwdriver, an adjustable wrench, pliers, and replacement parts like washers or O-rings. It's also helpful to have plumber's tape and a towel for cleanup.

Regular maintenance can prevent leaks. Avoid over-tightening handles, clean aerators periodically, and replace washers or seals at the first sign of wear. Additionally, installing a water softener can help reduce mineral build-up.

If the faucet is relatively new or of high quality and parts are available, repairing can be cost-effective. However, for old, corroded, or high-cost repairs, replacing the faucet might be more economical.
