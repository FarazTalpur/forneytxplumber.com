Frequently Asked Questions

High water pressure in a household is typically over 80 psi (pounds per square inch). Ideal water pressure should be between 40-60 psi to prevent damage to plumbing fixtures and appliances.

Common signs include banging pipes, running toilets, dripping faucets, and water hammer noises. Additionally, hoses and appliances may experience premature failure or leaks.

High water pressure can lead to leaking pipes, worn-out seals, and damaged appliances. It can also cause water hammer, which could damage pipes over time.

You can install a pressure reducing valve (PRV) to maintain safe pressure levels. Regularly monitoring your water pressure with a gauge and having routine plumbing check-ups can also prevent issues.
