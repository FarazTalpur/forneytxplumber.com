Frequently Asked Questions

Water pressure testing is a procedure used to measure the water pressure in the plumbing system of a building to ensure it is within the recommended range and identify any leaks or blockages.

Water pressure testing is important because it helps detect leaks, ensures appliances and fixtures function correctly, prevents damage to plumbing systems, and helps optimize water usage efficiency.

A water pressure test is typically performed using a pressure gauge connected to an outdoor faucet or laundry tap, and the system is checked for the pressure reading, leaks, and any fluctuations when water is running.

The ideal water pressure for a home is typically between 40 to 60 psi (pounds per square inch). Pressures over 80 psi can cause damage to plumbing and appliances, while pressures under 30 psi may result in insufficient water flow.

Yes, you can perform a water pressure test yourself by purchasing a water pressure gauge from a hardware store and attaching it to a faucet. However, if you're unsure about the results or find issues, it's advisable to consult a professional plumber.
