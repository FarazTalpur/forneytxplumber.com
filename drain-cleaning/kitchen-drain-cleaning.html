Frequently Asked Questions

It's recommended to clean your kitchen drain at least once a month to prevent buildup of grease, food particles, and other debris.

Baking soda and vinegar can be used to effectively unclog a drain. Pour half a cup of baking soda followed by half a cup of vinegar down the drain, wait 15-20 minutes, then flush with boiling water.

While chemical drain cleaners can be effective, they can also damage pipes if used frequently. It's generally safer to use natural cleaning methods or consult a professional for stubborn clogs.

Common causes of kitchen drain clogs include grease, food scraps like pasta and rice, coffee grounds, and fibrous vegetable peels.

To prevent clogs, avoid pouring grease down the drain and use a strainer to catch food particles. Regularly disposing of waste properly and running hot water through the drain can also help.
