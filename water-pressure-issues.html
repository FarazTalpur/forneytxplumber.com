Frequently Asked Questions

Low water pressure can be due to clogged pipes, a malfunctioning pressure regulator, or issues with the municipal water supply. Check your home's main shutoff valve to ensure it is fully open.

You can increase water pressure by adjusting the pressure regulator, cleaning out any clogged aerators or showerheads, and ensuring that no pipes are leaking. If needed, a professional plumber can assess and resolve more complex issues.

High water pressure may be caused by a fault in the pressure regulator if your home has one. It's also possible that the municipal supply exceeds the recommended levels. High pressure can damage plumbing fixtures, so it's essential to control it.

A water pressure booster can help if your home's water pressure is consistently low, and the municipality provides inadequate pressure. However, it is crucial to eliminate other possible causes of low pressure before installing a booster.

Fluctuating water pressure can be caused by air in the pipes, variations in the municipal water supply, or partially closed valves. Other culprits may include corroded or clogged pipes that need repair or replacement.
