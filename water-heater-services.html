Frequently Asked Questions

It is recommended to flush your water heater at least once a year to remove sediment build-up and improve efficiency.

Signs include inconsistent water temperature, reduced water flow, unusual noises, or rusty water. If you notice any of these issues, consider calling a professional.

The average lifespan of a conventional water heater is 8 to 12 years, but with proper maintenance, it can last longer.

If your water heater is over 10 years old and requires frequent repairs or is not energy efficient, replacement is often more cost-effective in the long run.

Tankless water heaters are more energy-efficient, provide endless hot water, save space, and have a longer lifespan compared to traditional tank heaters.
